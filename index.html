01 · About

Background &
Expertise

I am a Doctor in Industrial Cybersecurity (Mondragon Unibertsitatea), with a research stay at University College London (UCL) working with Prof. Justyna Petke and Prof. David Clark at the CREST centre — one of the leading software engineering research groups in Europe.

My work sits at the intersection of firmware security and hardware observability. I designed four research frameworks (CARNYX, GJALLARHORN, GAFLERNA, TRENTI) that integrate electromagnetic and power side-channel signals into fuzzing campaigns, enabling vulnerability detection on embedded targets without source code access or binary modification — directly applicable to IoT, automotive, and industrial control systems.

Before my PhD I worked at Ericsson on 5G core network security, at DNV on protocol testing for energy-sector devices, and at Barcelona Supercomputing Center on FPGA acceleration. I hold two MSc degrees (Cybersecurity + Telecommunication Engineering) from Universidad Carlos III de Madrid.

02 · Research

Frameworks &
Projects

Framework I

CARNYX

Power side-channel analysis integration for embedded fuzzing. Establishes the foundation for hardware-observable coverage-guided testing on constrained targets.

Framework II

GJALLARHORN

Electromagnetic measurement infrastructure for IoT targets. Enables non-intrusive EM signal acquisition during firmware execution to capture hardware-level behaviour.

Framework III

GAFLERNA

EM-guided fuzzing integration closing the loop between electromagnetic side-channel feedback and AFL++ mutation strategies. Extended journal version submitted to TOSEM / FSE 2025.

Framework IV

TRENTI

Multimodal in-loop feedback for embedded fuzzing, combining EM, power, and timing channels simultaneously. Submitted to IEEE EuroS&P. Includes cross-modal triangulation for hardware vulnerability diagnosis.
