01 · About

Background &
Expertise

I am a PhD candidate in Industrial Cybersecurity at Mondragon Unibertsitatea, supervised by Dr. Maialen Eceiza Olaizola and Dr. Mikel Iturbe Urretxa, with a research stay at University College London (UCL).

My doctoral work focuses on augmenting coverage-guided fuzzing with hardware-level observability — combining electromagnetic, power, and timing side-channel signals as runtime feedback to drive deeper exploration of embedded firmware targets.

Beyond academia, I bring industry experience from DNV (protocol testing), Ericsson (5G core security), and Barcelona Supercomputing Center (FPGA / HW-SW co-design). I hold two MSc degrees from Universidad Carlos III de Madrid in Cybersecurity and Telecommunication Engineering.

02 · Research

Frameworks &
Projects

Framework I

CARNYX

Power side-channel analysis integration for embedded fuzzing. Establishes the foundation for hardware-observable coverage-guided testing on constrained targets.

Framework II

GJALLARHORN

Electromagnetic measurement infrastructure for IoT targets. Enables non-intrusive EM signal acquisition during firmware execution to capture hardware-level behaviour.

Framework III

GAFLERNA

EM-guided fuzzing integration closing the loop between electromagnetic side-channel feedback and AFL++ mutation strategies. Extended journal version submitted to TOSEM / FSE 2025.

Framework IV

TRENTI

Multimodal in-loop feedback for embedded fuzzing, combining EM, power, and timing channels simultaneously. Submitted to IEEE EuroS&P. Includes cross-modal triangulation for hardware vulnerability diagnosis.
